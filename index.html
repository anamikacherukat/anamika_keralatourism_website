Kerala welcomes you!

On India's largest Malabar Coast, with nearly 600km of Arabian sea shoreline, lies the state of Kerala.
Being a state rich in heritage, culture and traditions, has always been one among to stand out in the crowd.
It has 14 districts with Malayalam being the most widely spoken language.
The name Kerala is derived from the Malayalam word 'kera' and 'alam', which stands for the 'The land of coconuts'.

Our Culture

The Kerala heritage holds a special and important place in the Indian history.
It is reflected in it's various art forms, martial arts, dresses, the people and cuisine.
It is well known for its scenic locations, architecture which is used to design its majestic temples, festivals like Onam, its variety of food and also its vast and rich Malayalam literature.

Our Cuisine


Kerala cuisine offers a wide variety of foods in vegetarian and non-vegetarian as well. It is a state rich with spices and so is the food delivered. Kerala is also known as the 'Land of Spices' The staple food revolves around an abundance of coconut, rice, tapioca and many spices. It is also famous for the delicious sea food, owing to the state's long coastline
