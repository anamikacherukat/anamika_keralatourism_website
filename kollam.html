God's Own Country

Welcome to Kollam

An iconic city that awaits with hidden adventures, idyllic backwaters and picturesque forest patches is Quilon or Kollam, as it is known today. Towards the south of God's Own Country is situated this pristine landscape on the banks of the Lake Ashtamudi, the southern gateway to the backwaters of Kerala.
Kollam narrates the tales of a bygone era of the Dutch, Portuguese and British rule. Being a prominent port in the legendary Spice Route, Kollam received much fame even during the time of explorers like Ibn Batuta and Marco Polo.
Venture beyond the well-known and experience the lesser known destinations that add to its enthralling aura. Discover a series of experiences from the alluring natural spectacles to historically significant edifices and ancient spiritual shrines.
